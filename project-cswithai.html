AI-Enhanced Learning Case Study

Learning CS with AI

A research-informed innovative instructional design project exploring the evolving roles of Generative AI in computer science education.

Project Overview

This project investigates the diverse andragogical roles Generative AI (GAI) can play to support CS learning. By moving beyond simple code generation, the curriculum designs activities where AI functions as a tutor, tutee, and co-creator.

The sequence follows an andragogical model that gradually reduces scaffolding, moving from directive prompting to self-regulated, metacognitive learning experiences.

Metacognition Learning Focus
Andragogy Instructional Model

Instructional Progression

1
AI as Tutor

High Scaffolding: Direct prompting instructions provided to students.

2
AI as Tutee

Learning by Teaching: Students act as tutors to the AI agent.

3
Collaborative Partner

Guided Iteration: Students iterate on prompts using Bloom's Taxonomy.

4
Co-Creator

Low Scaffolding: Designing culminating projects for teaching practice.

5
Self-Regulated Learning

No Scaffolding: Students design their own learning experience based on acquired AI-enhanced CS learning knowledge and skills.

Assignment Deep Dive

03. Prompting with Bloom's Taxonomy

Students transition to collaborative partners. They are tasked with designing and iterating on their own prompts, using Bloom's Taxonomy as a guideline to cover a wide range of cognitive domains (Remembering to Creating).

05. Self-Regulated Learning (SRL)

The final phase focuses on metacognition. Learners design their own Self-Regulated Learning experiences, documenting their thought processes, successes, and pivots when interacting with AI tools.
