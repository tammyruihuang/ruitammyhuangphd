AI-Enhanced Learning Case Study

Learning CS with AI

A research-informed innovative instructional design project exploring the evolving roles of Generative AI in computer science education.

Project Overview

This project investigates the diverse andragogical roles Generative AI (GAI) can play to support CS learning. By moving beyond simple code generation, the curriculum designs activities where AI functions as a tutor, tutee, and co-creator.

The sequence follows an andragogical model that gradually reduces scaffolding, moving from directive prompting to self-regulated, metacognitive learning experiences.

Metacognition Learning Focus
Andragogy Instructional Model

Instructional Progression

1
AI as Tutor

High Scaffolding: Direct prompting instructions provided to learners.

2
AI as Tutee

Learning by Teaching: Learners act as tutors to the AI agent.

3
Collaborative Partner

Guided Iteration: Learners iterate on prompts using Bloom's Taxonomy.

4
Co-Creator

Low Scaffolding: Designing culminating projects for teaching practice.

5
Self-Regulated Learning

No Scaffolding: Learners design their own learning experience based on acquired knowledge.

Assignment Deep Dive

3. Prompting with Bloom's Taxonomy

Learners design and iterate on their own prompts using Bloom's Taxonomy as a guideline to cover a wide range of cognitive domains.

4. AI Literacy Framework

Learners leverage the Stanford AI Literacy Framework to evaluate how AI tools can effectively support their culminating project plans.

5. SRL and Metacognition

Learners perform metacognition by designing and monitoring their own self-regulated learning experience while interacting with AI agents.
