Extended Reality

Project PHOENIX

Project PHOENIX is a collaborative initiative dedicated to pioneering participatory, human-centered methods for the design of extended reality (XR). I contributed to this project by leading Phase 1 and Phase 2, which involved the iterative co-design, development, and evaluation of XR environments for and with autistic users. My work focused on ensuring these advanced learning technologies are both equitable and inclusive by directly involving neurodiverse learners in the design process.

Games

Future Writers

Future Writers is an immersive, game-based learning intervention developed through a multi-phase Design-Based Research (DBR) approach. The project involved the iterative design, development, and formative evaluation of a 3D virtual world specifically engineered to foster Language-Related Episodes (LREs) among international English Language Learners (ELLs). By integrating task-based language teaching with immersive game mechanics, I created a collaborative environment where learners engage in meaningful social interaction and collective scaffolding to improve their communicative proficiency.
