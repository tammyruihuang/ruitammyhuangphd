Instructional Design Case Study

Differentiated CS Learning

Supporting diverse learners through a multi-tiered instructional design approach in foundational computer science programming.

Project Overview

Differentiated CS Learning is a strategic instructional design project implemented within Programming Foundations courses to bridge the gap in learners' varied entry points. This project acknowledges that students enter computer science education with a full spectrum of prior knowledge, professional experiences, and learning contexts.

By structuring all course module programming assignments into three distinct difficulty levels, I provide a personalized pathway for every student. This tiered approach allows beginners to build confidence while advanced learners are challenged with complex problem-solving tasks.

Design Artifacts

The Three-Level Assignment Framework

Level 1: Foundation

Core concept reinforcement for beginners or those transitioning into CS.

Level 2: Intermediate

Application-based tasks for learners with some prior programming experience.

Level 3: Challenge

Advanced logic reasoning and creative writing through code for high-proficiency learners.

Learning Path Design

Curriculum mapping that ensures all three difficulty levels meet the same fundamental learning objectives.

Personas & Needs Analysis

Developing tiered tasks based on common learner archetypes found in teacher professional development programs.

Refining through Action Research

I iteratively refine these tiered approaches by analyzing student submission data and feedback, ensuring the "Challenge" levels promote growth without being exclusionary.

Role & Instructional Impact

I designed and continuously refine this differentiated model for the Programming Foundations (JAVA) course at the University of Florida. This model is specifically grounded in Universal Design for Learning (UDL) and Learning Experience Design (LXD) principles to ensure accessibility for in-service teachers from underrepresented backgrounds.

My work involves conducting ongoing Action Research to evaluate how these tiered assignments influence teacher self-efficacy and their subsequent ability to broaden CS education in their own K-12 classrooms.
