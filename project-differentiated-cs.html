Instructional Design Case Study

Differentiated CS Learning

Supporting diverse learners through a multi-tiered instructional design approach in foundational computer science programming.

Project Overview

Differentiated CS Learning is a strategic instructional design project implemented within Programming Foundations courses to bridge the gap in learners' varied entry points. This project acknowledges that students enter computer science education with a full spectrum of prior knowledge, professional experiences, and learning contexts.

By structuring all course module programming assignments into three distinct difficulty levels, I provide a personalized pathway for every student. This tiered approach allows beginners to build confidence while advanced learners are challenged with complex problem-solving tasks.

Design Artifacts

The Three-Level Assignment Framework

Level 1: Foundation

Core skill implementation that enables beginners to practice and demonstrate mastery of the required skills.

Level 2: Intermediate

Extends core requirements by adding tasks that apply skills introduced in the current module context.

Level 3: Challenge

Integrates newly learned skills with prior concepts to design more complex and robust programs.

Refining through Action Research

I iteratively refine these tiered approaches by analyzing student submission data, reflections, and feedback. This ensures the program application context, scaffolding, and challenge levels promote growth without being exclusionary.

Role & Instructional Impact

I designed and continuously refine this differentiated model for the Programming Foundations (JAVA) course at the University of Florida. This model is specifically grounded in Universal Design for Learning (UDL) and Learning Experience Design (LXD) principles to ensure accessibility for in-service teachers from various backgrounds.

My work involves conducting ongoing Action Research to evaluate how these tiered assignments influence teacher self-efficacy and their subsequent ability to broaden CS education in their own K-16 classrooms.

Impact Highlight

By providing multiple pathways to success, this design reduced entry barriers for teachers with zero prior coding experience while maintaining high rigor for technical career-switchers.
