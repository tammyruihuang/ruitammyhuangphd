Game-Based Learning Case Study

Future Writers

An immersive 3D virtual world intervention designed to foster meaningful social interaction and language-related episodes among international English language learners.

Project Overview

Future Writers is a Digital Game-Based Second Language Learning (DGBL2L) environment developed as the centerpiece of my doctoral dissertation. The project addresses the need for immersive, task-based environments that encourage learners to consciously reflect on their language use.

The intervention utilizes a 3D virtual world to elicit Language-Related Episodes (LREs), providing international English Language Learners (ELLs) with a safe space for collaborative scaffolding and communicative practice.

Role & Research Impact

I led the end-to-end design and research of Future Writers, applying a Design-Based Research (DBR) approach across multiple phases of prototyping and evaluation. This work involved the conceptualization of meta-principles for DGBL2L interventions and the creation of an adapted activity-theory-based model for analysis.

The study demonstrated how balancing pedagogical design with hedonic game aspects can effectively promote language learning through meaningful social interaction and collective scaffolding.
