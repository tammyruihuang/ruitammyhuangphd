Extended Reality (XR) Case Study

Project PHoENIX

Pioneering participatory, human-centered methods for the design of inclusive extended reality (XR) environments for and with autistic users.

Project Overview

Project PHoENIX (Participatory, Human-centered, Equitable, Neurodiverse, Inclusive, eXtended reality) addresses the critical lack of autistic representation in immersive technology design.

The project utilizes co-design and participatory design within a learning experience design (LXD) frame to place autistic individuals, caregivers, and providers centrally in the processes of immersive technology development and research execution.

Role & Research Impact

I led the Phase 1 and Phase 2 iterative co-design, development, and evaluation of Project PHoENIX's immersive environments. This involved conducting empathy interviews, co-designing personas, developing 3D virtual world prototypes, and conducting formative usability evaluations.

My work grounded the design in a three-dimensional usability lens, ensuring that the technological, pedagogical, and sociocultural needs of autistic learners were addressed concurrently throughout the development lifecycle.
